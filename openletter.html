Here it is, an open letter. I have usually withheld criticism from pastors and preachers, with the concept that "I shouldn't criticize another man's (person's) ministry", because in the end, if that ministry is leading people to Christ, who am I to criticize it? However, after 2 years of being in attendance ad IDMJI, I have encountered multiple issues within the church as a whole. To be clear, I often find individuals within the church some of the most kind, respectful, God honoring people, however, at its core, this church has many issues that I will begin to lay out. Some of the issues are small, some are innaccuracies. Often these are not intentional (in my opinion) but rather, the nature of attempting to answer questions "immediately" and with short time constraints. - Let's Dive into this first thought. 1. Answering Biblical questions as they are asked within a few moments of the question being asked, is difficult, and in my opinion should be avoided. - Why? - 1. Time Constraint - The Q&A Sessions are rarely more than 1/2 an hour, some of the questions posed themselves could take up that amount of time to answer, as the questions can be complex. 2. Context IS everything. Often single verses, or groups of verses are questioned, and familiarizing oneself with the context of the verses are ABSOLUTELY necessary, I find it difficulty to always remember the context of a verse, I often have to take time to read the chapters before and after when understand what is being said, and who it is being said to, this takes time, and goes back to the first issue. 3. Memory/Understanding can be flawed - Memory of the meaning of a verse is not perfect, this is human, there have been a number of times I read a verse, and at first thought I understood it, only to later read, with more context or notes, or reading the original language and find out it meant something else, this points back to #2. In my opinion, biblical questions and answers isn't bad, but to me, the congregation should submit questions and then later those answered with proper time to study and digest. - What I find within IDMJI is often the answers are 1. a general summary. - this isn't bad, but sometimes key understanding is withheld. 2. dismissed entirely - sometimes the answer is "who are we to question the bible in this" , I find this strange. 3. sometimes fair, but again the practice itself of answering "on the fly" is something difficult at very least. 2. There is a near "worship" of Maria Louisa Piriquiva, there have been times when in QA she was asked if she "was an angel", there are times when people state that the church is "the one true church" or her teachings "the true doctrine". Often, members speak of her appearing in dreams, or prophecying within dreams. These are rarely rebuttled or rebuked.
