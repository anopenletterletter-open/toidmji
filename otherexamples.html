The Language of Comparison"Resembling" vs. "Being": Hebrews 7:3 explicitly says Melchizedek was "made like" or "resembling" the Son of God. Critics from Catholic Answers and Sound City Bible Church argue that if he resembles Jesus, he cannot logically be Jesus—just as a photograph resembles a person but is not the person.The "Order" of Priesthood: Scripture states Jesus is a priest "after the order of" Melchizedek (Psalm 110:4, Hebrews 5:6). Scholars at Answers in Genesis point out that an "order" implies a category; saying "Jesus is in the order of Jesus" is redundant and makes no linguistic sense.2. Genealogical DiscrepanciesLiterary vs. Literal Silence: Melchizedek is described as "without father or mother" (Hebrews 7:3). While Piraquive takes this literally, most theologians at places like Reformed Theological Seminary argue this is a literary device. Genesis intentionally omits his genealogy to make him a "living parable" of eternity, whereas Jesus’ genealogy is meticulously recorded in Matthew and Luke.The Incarnation Conflict: Jesus was born of a woman (Mary) and had a human lineage. If Melchizedek was Jesus appearing as a man, it would mean Jesus "became flesh" twice, which contradicts the New Testament teaching that the Incarnation was a unique, one-time historical event (John 1:14).3. Functional LogicThe Superiority Argument: Hebrews 7 argues that Melchizedek is a "type" because he is superior to Abraham. If Melchizedek is literally Jesus, the comparison loses its force—the author is trying to show how the office of priest-king is higher than the Levitical priesthood, using a historical human figure to prove it.Distinct Persons: Hebrews 7:15 refers to Jesus as "another priest" who arises in the likeness of Melchizedek. Using the word "another" (Greek: heteros) strongly implies a distinct person rather than the same individual appearing twice.4. Broader Practical ConcernsExclusivist Claims: Critics on Reddit and in reports from BuzzFeed News have noted that this specific doctrine is often used within the CGMJCI to reinforce the unique authority of Piraquive’s "divine revelations," which some defectors describe as a tool for control and spiritual manipulation
